### 2. **Professional-Grade Analysis**

The Ark analyzes **7 dimensions** that industry tools miss:

#### a) **Loudness Consistency** (MISSING from all competitors)
- Measures how steady loudness is over time
- LUFS/peak tell you average, not stability
- **The Ark detects:** "Volume riding" effect, inconsistent sections
- **Real-world impact:** Sounds professional on streaming vs "shiny"

#### b) **Frequency-Specific Dynamics** (MISSING from LANDR, Spotify)
- Which frequency bands are over-compressed?
- Sub-bass over-compressed but mids perfect = sounds weird
- **The Ark detects:** "Bass is crushed (2dB range), mids are dynamic (12dB range)"
- **Real-world impact:** Tonal imbalance, unnatural sound

#### c) **Mono Compatibility** (MISSING from all competitors)
- How much energy lost when played in mono?
- Invisible to stereo analysis
- **The Ark detects:** "Loses 35% energy in mono (beware: won't work in mono systems)"
- **Real-world impact:** Collapses on car stereos, mono playback systems, club systems

#### d) **Phase Coherence** (Pro tools have basic checks, The Ark explains)
- L/R phase relationship quality
- **The Ark detects:** "L channel 2.5dB louder, pulls image hard left"
- **Real-world impact:** Off-center sound on headphones

#### e) **Transient Analysis** (MISSING - only expensive transient shapers have this)
- How sharp are drums? How well-defined are attacks?
- **The Ark detects:** "Transients 34% too dull (need +3dB presence range)"
- **Real-world impact:** Sounds muddy/pillowy vs punchy

#### f) **Clipping Risk Prediction** (MISSING - only iZotope RX checks this way)
- What's the probability of clipping during:
  - Re-encoding (Spotify, YouTube)?
  - Loudness normalization?
  - On consumer playback systems?
- **The Ark detects:** "87% clipping probability on earbuds during streaming re-encode"
- **Real-world impact:** Distortion on delivery platforms

#### g) **Frequency Masking** (Pro mastering engineers do this by ear)
- Which frequencies are buried/masked?
- Only visible to trained ear or expensive FFT analysis
- **The Ark detects:** "200Hz + 4kHz masked by midrange (separate with surgical EQ)"
- **Real-world impact:** Lost definition, less clarity
